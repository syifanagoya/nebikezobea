Park Jae-sang, better known by his stage name
Psy, is a well-known South Korean record producer, rap artist, television personality, singer and songwriter, as well as an actor and actor-director. Psy is probably most known to the general public for his K-pop hit song “Gangnam Style,” which became a worldwide hit after being released in 201When it was first released in 2012, the song “Gangnam Style” rapidly gained popularity on the YouTube network, and it soon rose to the top of the music charts in 26 countries, including Canada, Germany, the United Kingdom, and Russia. The song had a significant cultural and social effect since it sparked a wave of “Gangnam Style” flash mob performances all over the globe, and it was even lauded by President Barack Obama of the United States of America. Sports stars like as Novak Djokovic, Chris Gale, and Manny Pacquiao have all done the famous dance at different events, and the song has appeared on television programmes such as “The X Factor” and “Glee.” It was also included in the Guinness Book of World Records in 2012 for receiving the most likes on its YouTube video, which was a first for the Korean pop sensation. Up to this point, more than five million copies of the song have been sold in the United States alone, earning it a Platinum certification from the Recording Industry Association of America.
