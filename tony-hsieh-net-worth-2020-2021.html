Early life

Born in the year 1973 on the monthly December, the birthplace of Tony Hsieh is Illinois. His parents are Richard Hsieh and Judy Hsieh. Both of them belong to Taiwan. Tony Hsieh spent his childhood days in California, San Francisco Bay area, and currently is the resident of Nevada, Las Vegas.

He is the elder brother of Dave Hsieh and Andy Hsieh. In the year 1995, Tony Hsieh became a graduate of Harvard University. He has a degree in computer science. While studying at Harvard University, Tony used to sell pizza and manage the Quincy House Grille. The most loyal customer of Tony was Alfred Lin, who later on became the COO and CFO of Zappos.

After graduating, Tony became an employee at Oracle Corporation, where he served for five months before realizing that he was not satisfied with this job. After quitting Oracle, Tony founded Link Exchange with another ex-employee of Oracle, Sanjay Madan. Then he sold this organization to Microsoft in 1998.

In 2010, a book named Delivering Happiness was released by Tony Hsieh, which is based on his entrepreneurship skills. This book was profiled in many popular publications, including The Wall Street Journal and The Washington Post. The book became a number one seller and conquered the number one post on the New York Times bestseller list.
