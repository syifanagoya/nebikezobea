Who Was Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya?

Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya Wiki: - Sir Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya was an Indian civil engineer, administrator, and statesman.  He is also known by MV. From 1912 to 1918, he held the position of 19th Dewan of Mysore. The line "the maker of modern Mysore" is frequently used to describe him.

During his career, Visvesvaraya worked for the Government of British India in the Middle Eastern territories of the Bombay Presidency and other British possessions. In 1885, Visvesvaraya was hired by the Public Works Department, Bombay, in the Bombay Presidency. Later, he was hired as the Laxmi Talav Dam's main engineer, close to Kolhapur.

On Dewan Sir V.P. Madhava Rao's invitation, Visvesvaraya joined the Mysore State as the chief engineer in November 1909. Visvesvaraya was chosen to serve as the Dewan of Mysore by Maharaja Krishnaraja Wadiyar IV in 1912. In Mysore Railways, he inaugurated various new rail lines.

Mokshagundam Visvesvaraya Achievments

  • In 1911, King Edward VII made Visvesvaraya a Companion of the Order of the Indian Empire (CIE).
  • In 1955, Visvesvaraya was awarded the Bharat Ratna, the highest civilian honor in India.
  • In his honor, Visvesvaraya Technological University in Belagavi, which is connected with the majority of engineering colleges in Karnataka, was founded.
  • His alma mater, the College of Engineering in Pune, dedicated a statue in Visvesvaraya's honor.
  • A memorial for Visvesvaraya is managed by the Visvesvaraya National Memorial Trust at Muddenahalli, the city where he was born.
  • In commemoration of Visvesvaraya's 158th birthday, a Google Doodle was created on September 15, 2018.
